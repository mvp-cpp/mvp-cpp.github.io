Genome-Wide Association Study · Rebuilt from rMVP to C++

MVP brings memory-efficient, visualization-enhanced, and parallel-accelerated GWAS into a modern C++ stack.

MVP is the C++ reimplementation of the original rMVP project. It keeps the core philosophy of handling large-scale GWAS efficiently, while moving toward a more performant, portable, and engineering-oriented software architecture for next-generation genomic analyses.

Scientific outputs

Publication-ready visualizations remain a first-class feature.

The original rMVP site emphasizes output figures such as phenotype distribution, SNP-density plots, PCA plots, circular and rectangular Manhattan plots, and Q-Q plots. The new MVP homepage keeps those strengths visible and places them at the center of the product identity.

  • Rectangular and circular Manhattan plots for GWAS result interpretation
  • PCA-based population structure visualization
  • Q-Q plots for model assessment and signal calibration
  • Distribution and density figures for fast exploratory analysis
